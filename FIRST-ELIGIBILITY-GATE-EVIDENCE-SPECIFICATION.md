# First Eligibility-Gate Evidence Specification

Status: **working research specification - not a gate, proposal, decision, or
activation**

Authority: this Lane 1 artifact describes evidence that would be needed before
Joe could responsibly consider a future eligibility gate. It does not amend
governance, make anyone eligible, open contribution, satisfy a gate, or commit
Joe to considering or approving one.

## Analytical Choice

This specification examines only the first eligibility class:
**authoritative project effort**.

That is an analytical choice, not an eligibility decision. Effort is examined
first because a future tightly bounded research contribution can be separated
from project decision voice and meta-governance voice more clearly than either
voice class can be separated from authority. This reasoning is
**Hypothesized**, not established evidence that effort eligibility should
expand first.

Decision voice and meta-governance voice remain outside this specification.
Joe remains the only eligible person in all three classes.

## Evidence Question

What evidence would Joe need before he could decide whether Purity Protocol is
capable of admitting one or more people to perform narrowly bounded,
authoritative research effort without granting them decision voice,
meta-governance voice, protocol standing, or permission for live activity?

This specification tests whether that question can become decision-legible. It
does not answer whether expansion is desirable or safe.

## Hypothetical Role Boundary

For evidence design only, the candidate role is a **bounded research
contributor**. A future gate would need to define the role at least this
strictly:

- work is limited to a named research question and approved public inputs;
- outputs are drafts with explicit claim labels and preserved provenance;
- no output changes repository authority, accepted decisions, Lane control,
  public claims, or project status until separately reviewed and accepted;
- the role confers no decision voice, meta-governance voice, participant
  standing, title, economic right, account, credential, or permission to
  represent the project;
- no contact with participants, collection of real participant data, live
  incentive, field activity, deployment, publication, or spending is implied;
  and
- authorization may stop prospectively, while already created work and its
  consequences remain subject to the rights and reversibility analysis below.

These are construction constraints for studying a possible gate. They are not
active role terms or an invitation to contribute.

## Required Evidence Bundles

A future gate should not be decision-ready until all eight bundles below have
named evidence, gaps, and a clear disposition.

### 1. Role and authority separation

Required evidence:

- a task schema that distinguishes bounded effort from project decisions and
  meta-governance;
- an acceptance path showing who may review, reject, revise, or adopt work;
- examples of outputs that stay inside the role and counterexamples that cross
  into voice, representation, or live operation; and
- a conflict analysis showing that repository visibility, licensing,
  relationships, or automation cannot silently confer the role.

### 2. Qualification and evidence integrity

Required evidence:

- criteria tied to the named research task rather than status, affiliation, or
  broad identity;
- a method for verifying provenance, construction choices, uncertainty, and
  negative results;
- challenge cases for plagiarism, fabricated citations, model-generated
  evidence, hidden conflicts, strategic omission, and confident overclaiming;
  and
- a review method that can reject weak work without converting reviewer
  preference into unrecorded policy.

No current qualification criteria are proposed here.

### 3. Metabolic capacity

Required evidence:

- the review, correction, security, and coordination load created by one
  bounded contributor;
- the repository's capacity to give timely acceptance, rejection, and
  correction without obscuring Joe's current authority;
- failure cases in which review backlog, ambiguous ownership, or accumulated
  drafts make the project less coherent; and
- a reasoned upper bound for the exact gate being considered rather than an
  open-ended contributor category.

### 4. Rights, provenance, and legal readiness

Required evidence:

- an adopted documentation and specification license or a reason the proposed
  work does not require one;
- contribution provenance, attribution, and disclosure rules;
- a patent posture appropriate to the work;
- treatment of pre-existing, jointly created, model-assisted, and third-party
  material; and
- any legal review needed before those terms can be relied on.

These questions are currently **Unknown**. Repository code's MIT license does
not settle them.

### 5. Consent, exit, review, and appeal

Required evidence:

- a plain-language disclosure of authority, scope, uncertainty, review,
  rejection, correction, and stopping conditions;
- affirmative opt-in by both the project and the candidate under their own
  authority;
- an exit process that states what records, drafts, attribution, obligations,
  and portable creation remain;
- a bounded review and appeal path that cannot silently create decision voice;
  and
- treatment of withdrawal when downstream reliance or public Git history
  cannot actually be erased.

### 6. Capture and integrity threat model

Required evidence:

- threats from collusion, sybil identities, reputation laundering, strategic
  free work, agenda capture, citation capture, social pressure, exclusion,
  hidden sponsorship, and privileged information;
- threats from access asymmetry, contributor dependence, founder bottlenecks,
  and reviewer concentration;
- at least one bounded synthetic or tabletop test for each material threat;
  and
- preserved no-go results and residual risks, not only mitigations.

### 7. Observability and stop evidence

Required evidence:

- observable leading signals for review load, correction latency, provenance
  quality, unresolved conflict, scope pressure, and authority confusion;
- explicit stop signals that pause new work before harm is compounded;
- a review interval and named deciding authority;
- a record format that separates signal from opaque scoring; and
- evidence that stopping the role would not be misrepresented as reversing
  every consequence already created.

Numeric thresholds should be proposed only after their measurement boundary
and failure meaning are justified. This artifact invents none.

### 8. Separate decision and activation proof

Required evidence:

- a complete gate proposal citing the other seven bundles;
- an explicit list of unresolved risks and evidence that would falsify the
  recommendation;
- a Joe decision on the gate itself;
- a separate Joe decision on whether the gate has been satisfied and whether
  any named eligibility should activate; and
- repository state that continues to show all eligibility closed unless and
  until every required decision is ratified through the governing authority
  layer.

## Real-Reversibility Evidence Matrix

| dimension | what a future gate must prove before activation | what stopping cannot be assumed to reverse |
|---|---|---|
| Repository access | least privilege, bounded duration, revocation test, and recovery path | knowledge already seen, copied public material, or leaked credentials |
| Work acceptance | drafts remain non-authoritative until named review and acceptance | reliance, reviewer time, or reputational effects of submission |
| Data and records | allowed inputs, retention, correction, export, and deletion limits | public Git history, third-party copies, or lawful retention duties |
| Economic | no implied payment, token, ownership, or future claim; any exception separately governed | uncompensated reliance, opportunity cost, or downstream value |
| Reputation and representation | no project title or speaking authority; correction path for misrepresentation | impressions already formed or third-party statements |
| Governance | no decision or meta-governance voice; audit trail for boundary crossings | informal influence, precedent, or social dependence |
| Consent and exit | bilateral opt-in, prospective stop, attribution and portability terms | consequences that cannot be restored by ending access |
| Attributable creation | ownership, license, credit, retained copies, and fork treatment are explicit | rights already granted or inseparable joint creation |

A software or access rollback alone is not sufficient evidence of real
reversibility.

## Disconfirming and No-Go Evidence

Any future gate should remain unsatisfied if evidence shows:

- bounded effort cannot be kept distinct from decision or meta-governance
  voice;
- rights, attribution, license, or patent posture is unresolved for the
  proposed work;
- review and correction load exceeds demonstrated repository capacity;
- the project cannot detect or stop authority confusion, capture, or material
  provenance failure;
- consent, exit, appeal, or retained-creation treatment depends on promises
  the project cannot keep;
- stopping relies on erasing public history or repairing consequences that are
  not actually reversible; or
- the evidence depends on live participants, incentives, deployment, or other
  activity not separately authorized.

Negative results are durable learning. They should narrow or kill a candidate
gate rather than be reframed as progress toward activation.

## Evidence Grades

Evidence should be labeled:

- **Established here:** current repository authority or directly inspectable
  repository fact within a named scope;
- **Synthetic support:** a bounded model, fixture, or tabletop result whose
  construction choices and limits are explicit;
- **External support:** relevant prior art with source, transfer limits, and
  disagreement recorded;
- **Disconfirming:** evidence against a requirement, mitigation, or gate;
- **Unknown:** missing or unjustified evidence.

No aggregate score may hide an **Unknown** rights dependency, a failed stop
condition, or disconfirming evidence.

## Current Disposition and Next Research

Current disposition: **not decision-ready**.

The rights/provenance bundle is the earliest hard dependency because outside
authoritative effort can create legal and retained-creation consequences even
when it creates no governance voice. A bounded next inquiry should compare
documentation/specification licensing and contribution-provenance options,
then use that result to refine the consent and real-reversibility bundles.

Joe retains every gate, satisfaction, and activation decision. Until a
separate ratified decision changes repository truth, no non-Joe person is
eligible to contribute authoritative effort, decision voice, or
meta-governance voice.
