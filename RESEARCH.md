# Phase 0 Research Agenda

Status: open questions and hypotheses; not settled protocol design

## Research discipline

Every inquiry should state its question, evidence boundary, construction
choices, claim status, result, negative evidence, and disposition. Research
feeds the Phase 0 graduation packet but cannot ratify itself or create
authority.

Use explicit claim labels:

- **Established here:** supported by repository evidence within a named scope.
- **Being tested:** active bounded inquiry with a stated method.
- **Hypothesized:** plausible founding idea without sufficient evidence.
- **Unknown:** no current justified answer.

## Mechanism and signal

- What does "better signal" mean in each intended context?
- When do pairwise comparison, confidence elicitation, peer prediction,
  forecasting, or truth-telling measures produce useful distinctions?
- How do sybil behavior, collusion, farming, conformity, strategic ignorance,
  unequal information, and model manipulation affect them?
- How should forecasting ability be distinguished from prediction, truthful
  reporting, expertise, or conformity?
- What constraints make paid participation productive rather than noisy?

## Funding and value

- Can system-native issuance or inflation sustainably fund informed attention?
- What is the unit of account, and who may legitimately define or measure
  collective increasing return?
- How do dilution, rent seeking, extraction, unequal access, treasury capture,
  and Goodhart effects change the result?
- Which non-monetary resources - recognition, access, opportunity, or voice -
  can be allocated with the same substrate?
- When are taxes, fees, subscriptions, donations, or other funding models
  superior?

## Governance and capture resistance

- What evidence shows that an eligibility expansion stays within metabolic
  capacity?
- Which rights and constraints should be constitutionally hard to change, and
  which belong to lighter policy or evolving precedent?
- Can a constraint-first, negative-possibility design preserve adaptability?
- What are the leading and stop indicators for corruption, collusion,
  exclusion, incoherence, and capture?
- How should the three eligibility classes transfer independently?
- Under what conditions is rollback real across software, data, economics,
  reputation, governance, and consent?

## Consent, exit, and portability

- What must informed opt-in disclose about authority, expected gates, timing,
  uncertainty, and exit?
- What constitutes attributable creation?
- What may a person retain or carry on exit or fork?
- How do names, records, reputation, data, obligations, and jointly created
  assets travel?
- What protections make exit credible without making collective commitments
  impossible?

## Rights and licensing

- Which license should cover documentation and non-code specifications?
- What contribution-provenance method should apply before outside contribution
  opens?
- Is an express patent grant necessary?
- How do open protocol rights relate to proprietary downstream applications?
- Which rights are legal-license questions and which require constitutional
  governance?

## Prior-art frames

Research should include, without treating any as gospel:

- Ostrom's social-ecological systems and institutional-governance work;
- public-goods funding and mechanism-design literature;
- quadratic mechanisms and their known attack surfaces;
- peer-prediction and information-elicitation mechanisms;
- prediction markets, forecasting, and calibration;
- cooperative game theory, including Shapley-value reasoning;
- constitutional design, common-law evolution, subsidiarity, and exit; and
- open-source governance and progressive-decentralization case studies.
