Status: ratified intent evidence and provenance; not a protocol specification

## Origin

Purity Protocol was established from Joe's 2026-07-23 governance and intention
interviews, a reconciliation of those interviews, and a bounded review of the
private `pure-os` repository. The private repository is source provenance, not
the new protocol's authority or history.

No private file or commercial material is reproduced here. The dispositions
below restate only the ideas Joe deliberately carried forward in public.

## Motivating reasoning

Decentralized decision-making can distribute power, but it becomes corruptible
when participants stop paying attention to the system they influence. Durable
collaboration therefore needs to reproduce the informed signal on which it
depends.

Markets align individual incentives effectively in many settings, yet public
goods and collective interests are often underprovided. Governmental and
institutional substitutes can themselves become bloated, captured, or
unresponsive. The founding hypothesis is that a collective might retain part
of the increasing return made possible by collaboration and use it to sustain
better ongoing signal, aligning an individual's best strategy with collective
improvement.

Paying for participation alone invites Goodhart effects and noise. Bitcoin is
relevant as evidence that open participation can be rewarded productively when
rules tightly constrain what counts. Peer-prediction and related methods may
help identify reliably informative participation without requiring an
omniscient answer key. These are starting hypotheses, not gospel.

The project also tests a governance claim: legitimate decentralization may be
more likely to emerge from explicit founder authority, observable transfer
gates, and evolving law than from pretending the project is decentralized on
day one. Expansion must stay within the institution's ability to metabolize
change without losing integrity.

## Deeper project principles

The project seeks positive-sum balance across individuals and collectives, now
and across different future horizons. It favors subsidiarity, voluntary
bilateral relationships, meaningful exit, forkability, contextual
accessibility, and graduated participation. Where trade-offs remain real,
legitimate governance must decide them visibly.

These intentions explain why the project exists. The Constitution and lawful
decisions determine what it may do.
