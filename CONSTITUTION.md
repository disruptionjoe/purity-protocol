# Purity Protocol Constitution

Revision: 1  
Status: ratified founding constitution  
Ratified by: Joe direct conversation, 2026-07-23

## Purpose, Passion, and Practice

**Purpose:** Make decentralized collective decision-making durable by building
informed attention into its economic structure, so individual participation
can reinforce collective increasing returns and sustain better, more
representative signal over time.

**Passion:** Discover whether legitimate decentralization can still be built
from the bottom up: founder-led and openly governed at inception, then
progressively transferred through explicit gates and evolving policy and law
without collapsing into either immediate vote-based capture or permanent
founder control.

**Practice:** Develop Joe's professional method for designing and stewarding
positive-sum institutions: turn mechanism-design convictions into explicit
constitutional rules, evidence-gated experiments, and credible transfers of
authority, then apply those methods across consulting, open-source
communities, and public-good systems.

## Constitutional principles

1. **Opt-in participation.** Every relationship is voluntary from both sides
   according to the authority mechanism of each side.
2. **Metabolic growth.** Inclusion should expand as far and as fast as the
   system can metabolize without compromising governance integrity, including
   second- and third-order effects.
3. **Positive-sum balance.** Governance should seek sustainable benefit across
   individuals, collectives, recursively larger collectives, and present,
   near-future, and continuing-future interests.
4. **Epistemic accessibility.** Access should be maximized contextually without
   losing the understanding needed to preserve signal integrity.
5. **Subsidiarity and bilateral consent.** Decisions should be made at the
   lowest competent level. Incompatible authorities cooperate only by mutual
   opt-in.
6. **Exit and forkability.** A party may exit. The project must research a
   meaningful right to exit with attributable creation rather than treating
   departure as forfeiture of everything created.
7. **Graduated openness.** Different forms of participation may have different
   eligibility. The intended direction is an open and permissionless protocol
   within integrity-preserving rules, not universal eligibility for every role
   at every stage.
8. **Constraint-first design.** Protocol law should presumptively constrain
   forbidden possibilities rather than prescribe one mandatory action. This is
   a provisional thesis, not a settled design conclusion.
9. **Legible authority and reversibility.** Authority, eligibility, transfer
   gates, and what can genuinely be reversed must be explicit at every phase.
10. **Governed evolution.** Future changes belong to the governance then in
    force, not to permanent unwritten founder prerogative.
11. **Plural implementations.** The protocol should be an open value-creating
    substrate. Downstream applications may use different business and
    licensing models without privileged protocol standing.

## Present authority and eligibility

Joe currently holds all repository governance authority. He is the only person
eligible to:

- contribute effort to authoritative project work;
- contribute voice to project decisions; and
- contribute voice to changing the rules governing eligibility and voice.

These are three separate eligibility classes. Public access, repository
visibility, source availability, a fork, or a relationship record confers none
of them.

## Authority hierarchy

Repository authority descends as follows:

1. this Constitution;
2. ratified decisions and constitutional amendments;
3. governance policy;
4. status and phase records;
5. roadmap;
6. public orientation in the README; and
7. conforming operational instructions in `AGENTS.md`.

Lower layers may implement, summarize, or narrow higher authority but cannot
silently amend it. Conflicts at one layer are resolved through the ratified
decision and supersession method, not by unexplained recency.

## Phase 0

Phase 0 establishes legible initial conditions for an evolving system. It must
produce:

1. a ratified constitution and authority hierarchy;
2. explicit rules for the three eligibility classes;
3. an evidence-gated progressive-decentralization roadmap;
4. constitutional and policy rule tiers with amendment processes;
5. an authority and reversibility matrix;
6. consent, exit, fork, and attributable-creation principles;
7. a research agenda for mechanisms, funding, capture resistance, and
   unresolved rights; and
8. a first eligibility-expansion gate.

Producing these outputs transfers no authority. Lane 1 may present a
graduation packet, but only Joe can decide whether Phase 0 graduates or whether
any eligibility expands.

## Amendment

This Constitution may be changed only by a formal amendment recorded in
`DECISIONS.md`. An amendment must identify the authority, reason, affected
rights and consequences, exact superseded text or rule, and effective date.
Emergency action, status edits, roadmap language, automation, or repository
activity cannot amend it.

## Current constitutional unknowns

Phase 0 must further define:

- the concrete progressive-decentralization gates and transfer sequence;
- the first eligibility-expansion gate;
- constitutional versus lighter policy rules beyond this founding division;
- an authority and real-reversibility matrix;
- operational consent, exit, fork, and attributable-creation rights;
- documentation and specification licensing;
- contribution provenance and patent posture; and
- the evidence needed to accept or reject founding mechanism hypotheses.
