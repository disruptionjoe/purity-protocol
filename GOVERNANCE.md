# Phase 0 Governance

Status: ratified governance policy under Constitution revision 1

## Current governor

Joe is the sole current governor and the sole eligible contributor in all three
eligibility classes: effort, decision voice, and meta-governance voice. This is
explicit founder governance, not distributed governance.

## Decision classes

| Class | Phase 0 method | Required record |
|---|---|---|
| Ordinary reversible | Joe may decide and record directly | Decision, reason, consequence, and claimed reversibility |
| Constitutional | Formal amendment only | Authority, reason, affected rights, exact supersession, consequence, effective date |
| Experiment | Joe approves a bounded design | Hypothesis, scope, consent boundary, stop conditions, evidence plan, and real rollback analysis |
| External or meaningfully irreversible | Separate explicit decision | Affected rights, consequences, dependencies, and reversibility limits |
| Emergency | Pause activity or restore the last safe state | Trigger, containment, preserved state, review, and follow-up decision |

Software rollback, data restoration, economic reversal, reputational repair,
governance reversal, and restoration of consent are different capabilities.
Calling a Git change reversible does not establish that its other consequences
can be reversed.

Emergency action grants no additional constitutional power. It cannot erase
history, permanently change the Constitution, admit participants, create a new
mechanism, or make an external commitment.

## Rule tiers

- Constitutional rules live in `CONSTITUTION.md` and change only by formal
  amendment.
- Ratified decisions and amendments live in `DECISIONS.md`.
- This file contains governance policy permitted by the Constitution.
- `STATUS.md` reports current fact and cannot create rights.
- `ROADMAP.md` expresses intended direction and cannot authorize work.

Phase 0 must research whether additional tiers are necessary and which rules
belong in each.

## Eligibility changes

No eligibility expansion is currently defined or open. Phase 0 must propose a
first gate that:

1. names the eligibility class or classes affected;
2. states who or what may qualify and how;
3. shows how the change stays within the project's metabolic capacity;
4. identifies rights, obligations, consent, exit, review, and appeal;
5. addresses capture, collusion, exclusion, and epistemic-integrity risks;
6. names measurable leading and stop indicators;
7. analyzes real reversibility and retained creation; and
8. requires a separate Joe decision to activate.

Research, a completed packet, or passing validation does not satisfy the gate.

## Phase 0 graduation

Lane 1 may present an evidence-supported graduation packet covering all eight
constitutional outputs and unresolved risks. Joe may approve graduation,
approve a narrower next gate, return the packet, defer it, or reject it.

Graduation does not itself admit a contributor, transfer authority, activate
an experiment, deploy anything, or begin live operation. Those remain governed
by the decision method in force.

## Relationship and fork posture

Relationships are bilateral and opt-in. Either side may narrow, suspend, or
exit according to the agreement and its own governance. Forking is a legitimate
response to irreconcilable policy. Phase 0 must define how attributable
creation, records, names, rights, and obligations travel or do not travel on
exit or fork.
